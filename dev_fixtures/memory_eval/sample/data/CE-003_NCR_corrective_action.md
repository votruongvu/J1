# CE-003 — Non-Conformance Report (NCR-007) and Corrective Action

Project: River Crossing Bridge Substructure
Document Type: NCR / Non-Conformance Report
NCR Number: NCR-007
Raised Date: 2026-03-23
Closed Date: pending
Originator: J. Pacheco, Civil Site Engineer
Quality Manager: A. Lin

## Issue / Defect

Concrete delivered to slab S-205, north pour zone (stations 1+250
to 1+280, approximately 30 m³) was below the specified C30/37
strength grade. Site cube tests at 7 and 28 days returned mean
characteristic strength values consistent with C25/30. The
specification section 03 30 00 requires C30/37 minimum for this
slab, and the corresponding BOQ rows 03.04 and 03.05 are priced
on that basis.

This NCR is the formal escalation of inspection Finding F-12
recorded in CE-002 (site inspection report). The defect was first
flagged during the routine concrete-grade verification inspection
on 2026-03-22 and confirmed by independent third-party laboratory
testing on 2026-03-23.

## Root Cause

The supplier coordinator dispatched the incorrect mix design for
the slab S-205 north pour. The delivery ticket recorded
"C30/37 RC2" but the batched mix was the supplier's standard
C25/30 product. The site team accepted the delivery without
checking the cube test schedule against the dispatch note. The
root cause is therefore a combination of supplier mis-dispatch
and a gap in the site acceptance procedure.

## Corrective Action

1. Selectively demolish the affected 30 m³ of slab S-205 north
   pour, working in 5 m³ lifts to control vibration on adjacent
   completed sections.
2. Re-pour with high-strength concrete grade C40/50 per BOQ row
   03.06. The increase in grade restores the structural margin
   originally specified for C30/37 once the demolition residue
   is accounted for.
3. Coordinate with the reinforcement detailer to confirm cover
   and lap requirements remain satisfied after demolition.
4. Update the as-built drawings to record the re-pour zone and
   the actual concrete grade installed.

## Preventive Action

* Update the site concrete acceptance procedure to require the
  site engineer to cross-check every delivery ticket against the
  batching plant's daily mix schedule before unloading begins.
* Add a sign-off slot for the supplier coordinator on the
  delivery ticket so dispatch responsibility is explicit.
* Schedule a refresher tool-box talk for the concrete crews on
  the consequences of accepting a non-conforming mix.
